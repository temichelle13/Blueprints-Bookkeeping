Website Audit for Blueprints & Bookkeeping
Overview

Blueprints & Bookkeeping (blueprintsandbookkeeping.com) positions itself as a boutique firm offering advanced bookkeeping, lender‑ready business plans and a digital‑presentation service it calls the “Digital Handshake.” The site is designed to appeal to founder‑led businesses and investors by emphasising precision, professionalism and limited capacity (20 active clients). Overall the design is sleek and on brand, but a detailed audit uncovers areas of functional improvement, legal compliance gaps and messaging that could be misinterpreted.

Technical Functionality & Usability
Navigation and Layout

Navigation bar: The primary menu (Home, About, Services, Industries, Pricing, Portfolio, Results, Blog, Get Started) is consistent across pages. The “Get Started” buttons and call‑to‑action (CTA) links correctly route to the contact form.

Responsiveness: Pages load quickly on desktop. However, testing on a narrow viewport (≤ 768 px) was not performed and should be validated to ensure mobile‑friendly layout and font sizes.

Forms: The “Submit Discovery Application” form on the contact page displays fields for services, description of challenges and contact details. Required‑field validation is not obvious; the form should prevent submission without mandatory fields and display user‑friendly error messages.

Broken links: Two critical links in the website’s footer—Privacy Policy and Terms of Service—lead to a 404 page with the message “Did you forget to add the page to the router?”

Design and Accessibility

Visual hierarchy: The homepage hero section emphasises the tagline and CTAs. The “Three Pillars of Growth” section clearly outlines the core offerings. Testimonials are presented as anonymised case studies.

Colour contrast & font: Most text is dark on light backgrounds, but grey text on light grey backgrounds (e.g., some captions) may not meet WCAG AA contrast requirements. Ensure all text has a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text.

Alt text: Images (e.g., case‑study diagrams, portfolio examples) did not display alt attributes in the page source. Adding descriptive alt text improves accessibility and search‑engine optimisation.

Keyboard navigation: The site should be tested to ensure all interactive elements (links, buttons, form fields) can be accessed via keyboard alone and include focus indicators.

Performance and Security

Loading speed: Pages load quickly; however, large hero images and multiple external fonts may increase load times on slow connections. Use image compression and lazy‑loading where appropriate.

SSL/TLS: The site loads over HTTPS (TLS 1.3) and displays a valid certificate; there were no mixed‑content warnings.

Cookie banners: No cookie‑consent banner appears. Depending on the geographical areas served, privacy regulations (e.g., GDPR) may require disclosure of tracking cookies or analytics.

Recommendations

Add missing legal pages. Create comprehensive Privacy Policy and Terms of Service pages that address data collection, cookie usage, liability, intellectual‑property rights and user obligations. Ensure these pages are linked in the footer and accessible from every page.

Strengthen professional disclaimer. On the footer and service pages, add a clear statement that Blueprints & Bookkeeping is not a CPA firm and does not provide tax, legal or investment advice. Encourage clients to work with licensed professionals for compliance matters.

Clarify service scope. 

Under Advanced Bookkeeping, explain the transaction volume and complexity levels included at different price points.

For Business Plans, specify deliverables (e.g., number of revisions, financial model depth) and note that funding outcomes depend on various factors beyond your control.

In Digital Handshake, clarify whether it is included with business‑plan engagements or offered separately.

For RON, provide a list of acceptable documents and a note about state‑specific restrictions.

Improve accessibility. Check colour contrast ratios, add alt text to all images, provide skip‑navigation links and ensure keyboard accessibility. Offer accessible PDFs or print versions of business plan websites for users who require them.

Enhance trust indicators. Add logos/badges with links to issuing bodies (e.g., QuickBooks ProAdvisor certification) and consider including client logos with permission. Provide a physical mailing address or at least a registered office address to meet legal requirements in some jurisdictions.

Content updates and blog strategy. Publish regular articles that address common bookkeeping challenges, regulatory updates and small‑business finance tips, always including a disclaimer. Encourage newsletter sign‑ups by offering a free downloadable checklist or template.

Compliance with email marketing laws. Ensure that newsletter subscription forms clearly indicate how subscribers’ data will be used and that unsubscribe links work (currently functioning). Double‑opt‑in may be required in some regions.

Security best practices. If collecting sensitive data (e.g., financial documents), implement end‑to‑end encryption or secure client portals. Use strong spam‑protection on forms (e.g., reCAPTCHA) to prevent automated submissions.

Conclusion

Blueprints & Bookkeeping’s website projects professionalism and expertise. The design is coherent, and services are well categorised. However, legal compliance gaps (missing privacy and terms pages), ambiguous wording around compliance and tax‑related matters, and some accessibility oversights should be addressed to avoid potential liabilities and to improve user trust. Implementing the recommendations above will strengthen credibility, protect the company from legal risk and enhance the overall user experience.
